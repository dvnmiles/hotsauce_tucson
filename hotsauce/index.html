Original

Here are the supplies and materials:

  • A bushel of ripe serrano perppers.
  • 1 Tablespoon of seasalt.
  • 2 cups of distilled water.
  • One 1 qt glass jar.
  • One air lock with sealing lid.
Here are the steps:
  1. Choose your favorite pepper(s). Either a single variety or a belnd of peppers will do.
    2. My favorite pepper for hotsauce is the serrano.
  2. Fill a 2-quart jar with the peppers.
    3. For added flavor and feel free to experiment with additional ingredients such as onions, garlic, and sliced carrot
  3. Perpare a salt water brine by dissovling 1.25 tsp. of seasalt per 1 cup of warm distilled water.
    4. The proper salt to water ratio is important. Too little salt can allow unhealthy bacteria to grow. Whereas, adding too much salt kills the bacteria and stops the fementaion process. If you need to add more water to the jar, then add salt accordingly.
  4. Pour the brine over the peppers until they are completly submerged.
  5. Place a glass fermenting weight on top of the peppers so it completly submreges all the peppers and itself under the waterline.
    6. carefully remove any floaters with a sterilized utensil
  6. Firmly screw lid on to the jar.
  7. Assembly and fill the waterlock.
  8. Insert waterlock into the center hole in the jar lid.
  9. Place the jar in a cool and dark place, such as the pantry or cupboard.
  10. Wait at least 7 days before returing to the brine.
  11. Puree the brine and pepper mixture.
  12. Strain the pulp using cheese cloth.
    13. spread the pulp out on a cookiesheet and allow it to dry for a lovely cutsom spice for the spice rack.
  13. Optional: Add --tsp. of citric acid as a preservative.
    14. may slightly affect flavor; however, some people like the subtle taste.
  14. Optional: Add --tsp. of xanthan gum to the hotsauce while blending to act as a thickening agent.
    15. 100% natural and netural flavor. use between 1/8 to 1/4 teaspoon depending on thickening preference.
  15. Bottle the hotsauce in the facny woozy bottles.
